Glossary

The government information we publish, much of it recorded by detention centre staff, is full of jargon and abbreviations. It can be hard to understand because it wasn't written with outsiders in mind. We hope the definitions you find here make Detention Logs more accessible and easier to approach.

If you think something is missing, incomplete or inaccurate, please let us know with an email to detentionlogs@gmail.com or via Twitter to @detentionlogs. Helping extend this glossary is an easy way to make this site more useful for everyone.

AFP
Australian Federal Police
AITA
Adelaide Immigration Transit Accommodation. This facility is located 5km from Adelaide CBD and is adjacent to Adelaide Airport. It comprises two three-bedroom domestic houses.
APOD
Alternative Place of Detention. This is a broad term used by the Department of Immigration and Citizenship to describe different forms of detention facilities and does not necessarily refer to permanent places of accommodation. The facilities referred to in this category can range from community housing, schools or hospitals for medical treatment.
ASOM
Banksia Compound
Banksia Compound is the female only section at Villawood IDC. It is within the Hughes accommodation area.
Berrimah House
Berrimah House for juvenile foreign fishers has a regular use operational capacity of 12 and a capacity to accommodate 16 during a surge of clients. It became operational in December 2008.
BITA
Brisbane Immigration Transit Accommodation
Christmas Island Construction Camp
The Construction Camp was first built in 2002 to accommodate construction workers for the new North West Point Facility on Christmas Island. It was converted to an Alternative Place of Detention in 2008 and is located adjacent to the Phosphate Hill facility.
Code Red
Fire hazard
Code Yellow
Self harm attempts.
Example: Incident 1–3DWZJJ, incident 1–3H6LHL.
Code Green
Detainee escape.
Example: Incident 1–3I11M1
Code Black
Threats of assault
Code Blue
Detainee injured or hurt
Example: Incident 1–2YYRDL
Code Purple
Minor disturbances and scuffles between clients
CIIDC
Christmas Island Immigration Detention Centre
The Christmas Island Immigration Detention Centre incorporates a main North West Point area purpose built for immigration detention and two additional compounds Aqua and Lilac comprising demountable buildings.
CSO
Serco Client Services Officer. This is the entry level position for Serco employees working at detention facilities. According to Serco job advertisements CSO’s are required “to work with clients in an empathetic manner, treating them with respect and dignity. Taking into account the client’s safety and wellbeing through maintaining a secure and stable environment by pro-active integration with clients on a daily basis.”
CSM
Serco Client Services Manager
DALAPOD
Darwin Airport Lodge Alternative Place of Detention
DAL3
Darwin Airport Lodge Stage 3
DPH
Darwin Private Hospital
Fowler
Fowler Compound, is an accommodation area at Villawood IDC. According to ABC’s Four Corners program Asylum (October, 2011), Fowler Compound is used to house, “high risk detainees – people at high risk to others or themselves.”
IDC
Immigration Detention Centre
IDF
Immigration Detention Facility. This broad term is used to describe a range of different categorisations of detention centres where people within the immigration detention network are held.
IHMS
International Health and Medical Services. This company retains the contract for the delivery of health services in immigration detention. The health provider is a related entity of International SOS, which is a multinational health services and medical evacuation company that has operations around the world. The contract for Australia’s immigration detention centres is valued at $272m and will expire in January 2014. In addition to this IHMS has also retained a $41m contract for regional processing areas, and a $21m contract for services on Manus Island and Nauru.
IRH
Immigration Residential Housing. This term is used to describe parts of some detention facilities where detainees live that are considered by the Department of Immigration and Citizenship to be more focused on community living.
ITA
LAPOD
Leonora Alternative Place of Detention
Lilac Aqua
Lilac Aqua is a detention facility located on Christmas Island.
MIDC
Maribyrnong Immigration Detention Centre. MIDC is a single-level detention facility constructed in the early 1980s that was extended and refurbished in 2005. MIDC was first opened in 1983 and is located in Maidstone, northwest of Mebourne CBD.
MITA
Melbourne Immigration Transit Accommodation. This immigration facility is a small residential building located just outside of the Melbourne CBD in Broadmeadows.
MSS
MSS Security. This company was subcontracted by Serco to deliver security services at Immigration Detention Facilities. MSS lost this contract with Serco in 2012. Wilson Security is now responsible for subcontracted security services in mainland detention centres.
NIDC
Northern Immigration Detention Centre. NIDC was originally constructed in 2001 as contingency for irregular maritime arrivals. The facility has an operational capacity of 382.
NWP
North West Point Immigration Facility. NWP is located on Christmas Island and was constructed in 2008. The facility expanded the scope of detention facilities on the Island. It is also known as Christmas Island IDC (Immigration Detention Centre).
PIDC
Perth Immigration Detention Centre. PIDC is a single-level secure detention facility that was adapted from an old administrative building. It was first opened as a detention facility in 1981. The building has separate wings for male and female detainees
PIRH
Perth Immigration Residential Housing
Phosphate Hill
Phosphate Hill is a temporary immigration facility situated approximately 5kms from the settled areas of Christmas Island, and is adjacent to the Christmas Island Recreation Centre. It’s designated as an Alternative Place of Detention and was first opened in 2001.
RDH
Royal Darwin Hospital
RRT
Refugee Review Tribunal. Asylum Seekers who have received an adverse decision from the Department of Immigration and Citizenship regarding their refugee claim can lodge appeal to the Tribunal.
s. 47F(1)
Redacted under the personal privacy exemption of the Freedom of Information Act 1982 (Cth). This provision has been exercised by the Department of Immigration and Citizenship to release documents, in most cases in relation to the identities of asylum seekers.
Serco

Serco Group plc is a British government services company based in Hook, North Hampshire in the United Kingdom. It is listed on the London Stock Exchange and is a constituent of the FTSE 100 Index. Among its operations are public and private transport and traffic control, aviation, military and nuclear weapons contracts, detention centres and prisons, and schools. Serco’s contract with the Immigration Department to supply detention services ends on 28 June 2014.

Text from DIAC > On 29 June 2009, DIAC entered into a five-year contract with Serco Australia Pty Ltd (Serco) to provide services at immigration facilities. Serco took responsibility for detention service provision through a staged approach beginning on Christmas Island on 1 October 2009. The transition from the previous detention service provider to Serco was completed on 31 January 2010 when Serco took responsibility for managing all immigration detention facilities on Christmas Island and mainland Australia.

SASH Watch
SIDC
Scherger Immigration Detention Centre. Scherger detention facility was opened in October 2010 and is a former RAAF base.
SIRH
Sydney Immigration Residential Housing
Sydney IRH is adjacent to [Villawood IDC](#link to villawood info) in western, Sydney, and comes under the same management, though it is often characterised as a separate facility. According to DIAC’s website ‘Placement in immigration residential housing allows people in immigration detention to live in a more domestic environment and permits a greater degree of autonomy over their life while remaining formally in immigration detention.’
SKSA
Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport. This location is referred to occasionally in the data we have because it’s where many international flights depart that detainees are deported on.
VHS
Voluntary Hunger Strike
VIDC
Villawood Immigration Detention Centre.

Villawood IDC, managed by Serco Australia Pty Ltd (Australian branch of the UK based multinational Serco Group), is located in western Sydney at 15 Birmingham Avenue, Villawood NSW 2163, and has an operating capacity of 379 detainees and a contingency capacity of 480 (as of 29th April 2013).

Villawood IDC has 3 main sections:

  • Blaxland (aka Stage 1)
  • Hughes (aka Stage 2), contains female-only area Banksia
  • Fowler (aka Stage 3), for “high risk detainees – people at high risk to others or themselves,” according to ABC’s Four Corners program Asylum (October, 2011)

Sydney Immigration Residential Housing (IRH) is adjacent to Villawood IDC and is also managed by Serco.

An immigration processing facility was first opened at Villawood in 1976. Prior to that time it was called the Westbridge Migrant Hostel, which was opened in 1946 to provide accommodation for new migrants. The facility was initially run by the Federal Government but management was outsourced in 2003 to G4S. In 2009 G4S lost the contract and Serco took over the facility.
