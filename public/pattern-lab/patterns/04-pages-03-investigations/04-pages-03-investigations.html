Investigations

These investigations draw on data published by Detention Logs or relate to the project. They cover broad areas of immigration detention including suicide, hunger strikes and deportations, and tell these stories in many different ways. They have been written by Detention Logs journalists and by other journalists from news organisations across Australia. Read our Reporting Recipe to find out how to approach this resource for your own investigation or research.

Deported by DIAC Into Perpetual Limbo

by Paul Farrell, Lawrence Bull and Luke Bacon

August 14, 2013 - Ziad Awad was bundled onto a plane to be deported from Australia in 2011. He had slashed his wrists and was gushing blood. That was just the beginning. He spoke to New Matilda and Detention Logs in Ankara, Turkey.

Asylum Seekers Deported with Violence

by Paul Farrell, Lawrence Bull and Luke Bacon

14 August, 2013 - The Department of Immigration has approved the use of a body lock, strongly resembling a restraint technique banned by police, to involuntarily deport people from Australia.

Assault, gang rape claims in detention rise

by Natalie O’brien

July 28, 2013 - The level of alleged gang rapes, sexual assaults, and sexual harassment of staff and detainees in detention centres has jumped dramatically as the number of asylum seekers arriving in Australia swells to a 10-year high.

July 23, 2013 - The contract for the management of Manus Island detention facility reveals that lower standards are expected for the quality of care of asylum seekers in Papua New Guinea.

Manus Oversight Committee Has Never Met, Immigration Department Admits

by Paul Farrell, Lawrence Bull, Luke Bacon and Oliver Laughland

July 15, 2013 - The body set up between Australia and Papua New Guinea to oversee the treatment of asylum seekers at Manus Island detention facility is yet to have a single meeting.

Salvos Worried About Nauru Chaos

June 25, 2013 - Salvation Army documents from 2012 reveal chaos at Nauru detention centre shortly after it was reopened. Detention Logs reports on dysentary, dog attacks and poor medical supplies.
